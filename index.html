Full-stack Skills

1. I built a UX foundation to support a growing product

Product: Zingtree no-code interactive decision tree software

Company: Zingtree Inc.

Problem

The Zingtree product originated as an all-purpose “do anything” decision tree. To take it to the next level, we focused it for the CX industry and Contact Centers. This required rethinking some UX paradigms without breaking things that worked well.

Solution

To understand CX industry users, I regularly conduct customer interviews to generate and evaluate design ideas. I initiated a contextual inquiry study to understand the product's users prior to a version migration. This uncovered critical issues that could be addressed prior to the upgrade.

2. I have transformed how Accountants research tax law

Project: “CCH AnswerConnect” Tax and Accounting research tool

Company: Wolters Kluwer U.S.

Problem

The U.S. Tax Code is over 74,000 pages in length and even the most knowledgeable accountants get lost, especially when navigating between content from different sources.

Solution

I broke all the information architecture rules of professional research tools (which have their origin in printed books) and reorganized content to meet the needs of users (which became obvious to me after I observed them conduct their work).

3. I’ve abandoned outdated business models to improve the customer experience

Project: “CCH iKnow” Tax and Accounting research tool

Company: Wolters Kluwer Australia

Problem

Many tax practitioners conduct research via Google, using keywords related to the issue at hand. But professional subscription-based tools don't allow search bots to crawl their content, so they can't be discovered serendipitously.

Solution (Award-winner*)

I designed a user experience that incorporates a freemium business model, making some content public and thus visible in Google. As users interact with the product, the “paywall” is incorporated to indicate the value of a paid subscription.

*Won an employer award for “Greatest Value to Customers”.

4. I’ve reimagined the taxpayer experience

Project: Tax Design Challenge

Client: U.S. Internal Revenue Service

Problem

Not all taxpayers know they can access their IRS data in a document called the “Tax Return Transcript”. Even if they do, the information can be difficult to understand, since it contains 200 data fields formatted for finance professionals, not individual taxpayers.

Solution (Award-winner*)

I redesigned the tax information in a way that makes it easier for taxpayers to manage their responsibilities and make effective decisions about their personal finances.

*Won a government award from the IRS for “Best Financial Capability” (second place).

5. I’ve reinvented desktop software for the web

Project: “TaxPrep” software cloud migration

Company: Wolters Kluwer Canada

Problem

TaxPrep is tax software used by virtually all of Canada’s top accounting firms, as well as the Canadian government. But its Windows-based interface is dated, and needs reinvention for the inevitable move to the cloud.

Solution

I created a design solution which retains the functionality of the legacy software, while also adopting modern, web-based UI.

6. I’ve brought new insights to Tax Practitioners

Project: “CCH iQ” Predictive Tax Intelligence tool

Company: Wolters Kluwer Australia

Problem

Accountants don’t always know how legislative changes and tax events might affect their clients, or how these changes could bring in new business.

Solution (Award-winner*)

I designed an interface which delivers insights built on cutting-edge technology (AI language parsing), without making the behind-the-scenes machinery visible—it just works.

*Won an employer award for “Greatest Value to Customers”.

7. I have made stock data more informative for millions of people

Project: Wall Street Journal Company Research

Company: WSJ.com

Problem

Stock and investing resources are full of data. Typically, this information is presented as numerical values, leaving readers guessing if a number is average or unusual.

Solution

I used visualization and reference points to give the reader insight and context into the stock they are researching.

(The product has been updated since I worked on it, but my data visualization design is intact.)
